What this is

A weekly-maintained, CSV-backed pipeline of CO jurisdictions where IndiBuild is actively pursuing housing development opportunities. The pipeline tracks the full lifecycle from "I noticed a signal" → "a contract is executed", plus the network of contacts, dropped targets, and re-usable outreach templates that support it. This page is read-only; data lives in the CSVs.

The 5 stages (left-to-right on the kanban)

  1. Screen — passed the Opportunity Finder filter (above the IOI threshold) but no signal logged yet. Default landing column for new entries.
  2. Signal — at least one moderate-or-strong signal logged in the last 90 days (HNA refresh, RFP open, dedicated funding move, etc).
  3. Outreach — email sent or call scheduled in the last 30 days. Move here as soon as a real conversation is in flight.
  4. IC — feasibility analysis or Investment Committee packet underway; specific site / partner identified.
  5. Active — executed LOI / MOU / contract. The actual deal is live.

Demotion rule: any jurisdiction sitting 90+ days in a stage with no progress gets pushed one stage left with a notes entry explaining why. A 6th stage, Monitor, exists in the CSV for long-cycle relationships and is folded into the Screen column on this kanban.

The IOI score

The IndiBuild Opportunity Index is a 0–100 composite computed by the LIHTC Opportunity Finder for every CO jurisdiction. It blends housing-need pressure, funding posture (Prop 123, PAB, dedicated revenue), civic capacity, basis-boost eligibility, and market-capture advantage. The full weighting is documented in LIHTC-LOCATOR-METHODOLOGY.md. We promote any jurisdiction above ~60 to at least the Screen column.

Classifications (A / B / C / D)

  • A — real development opportunity, IndiBuild as primary developer.
  • B — planning-to-development consulting opportunity; fee work that may convert to A.
  • C — relationship / monitor; long-cycle but worth nurturing.
  • D — not worth current time; moved to Anti-Targets.

Confidence (high / medium / low)

  • high — in the COHO curated 33 + recent ACS/CHAS + ZORI or Apartment List coverage.
  • medium — recent ACS/CHAS but limited policy data.
  • low — sparse data; small CDP; no recent HNA. A low confidence row should not be in Outreach without an explicit override note.

Signal strength (weak / moderate / strong)

  • weak — interesting but not actionable. Default for most rumors / generic council mentions.
  • moderate — actionable in 30–90 days (HNA draft expected, RFP cycle approaching, personnel change).
  • strong — drives a same-week action (open RFP < 60 days, immediate land disposition, direct partnership invite). If > 30 % of your signals are strong, you're inflating — recalibrate.

Where the cards link

  • The jurisdiction name in each kanban card links to its Jurisdiction Brief — a full data sheet with HNA status, AMI gap, LIHTC inventory, funding readiness, and civic scoring.
  • The Sources row at the bottom of each card links directly to the signal-log entries (and their original URLs) that justified the pipeline placement. If a card has no sources, that's a flag — promote a signal or demote the entry.

Weekly review routine

Documented in WEEKLY-REVIEW.md: Monday morning, walk the kanban left-to-right, demote stalled entries, promote new signals, add next_action + next_action_due for each active row, then sweep the signal log for anything older than 90 days.

Data files (all in docs/indibuild-pipeline-prototype/)

  • 01-signal-log.csv — every observed signal with date, source, source_url, strength.
  • 02-pipeline.csv — the kanban itself (one row per jurisdiction in the funnel).
  • 03-anti-targets.csv — explicitly dropped jurisdictions with reason + revisit date.
  • 04-network.csv — people we know in each jurisdiction.
  • 05-outreach-templates.csv — re-usable email/letter templates.
  • CONTROLLED-VOCABULARY.md — exact allowed values for every column. Edit this before using a new value.

Guard rails

Two CI jobs guard this data:

  • npm run test:indibuild-geoids — every geoid in the CSVs must resolve to a real Census place whose name matches the jurisdiction string. Catches fabricated geoids.
  • .github/workflows/indibuild-url-health.yml — weekly cron that pings every source_url in the signal log + every policy-progress URL; opens an issue when a link rots.
