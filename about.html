About LIHTC Analytics Hub

A comprehensive platform for understanding Low-Income Housing Tax Credit allocations, market dynamics, and affordable housing development across the United States.

Platform Purpose

The LIHTC Analytics Hub provides data-driven insights into how federal housing tax credits are allocated, priced, and deployed across states and metro regions. Our platform serves affordable housing developers, tax credit investors, housing finance agencies, and community development professionals.

We consolidate data from authoritative sources to help users understand policy variations, market conditions, and development opportunities in the affordable housing sector.

Why LIHTC Matters

The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit program is the federal government's primary tool for encouraging private investment in affordable rental housing. Since 1986, LIHTC has financed approximately 3.6 million affordable rental units across all 50 states.

While federally authorized, LIHTC operates through a state-level allocation system. Each state housing finance agency designs its own Qualified Allocation Plan (QAP), creating distinct development environments, risk profiles, and investment outcomes across regions.

Methodology

Data Aggregation: We collect data quarterly from authoritative sources and validate it against multiple references to ensure accuracy.

State Allocations: Per-capita calculations use Census Bureau population estimates. Allocation tiers are based on total dollar amounts, not adjusted for cost of living or construction costs.

Market Analysis: Pricing data reflects secondary market transactions and may vary by deal structure, investor type, and state-specific factors.

Economic Indicators: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) provides standardized metrics updated according to each series’ publication schedule.

Legislative Impact Modeling: Estimated cost savings based on analysis of H.R. 6644 provisions compared to current regulatory requirements. NEPA streamlining timeline reductions based on HUD environmental review data.

Commodity Forecasting: Forward projections combine futures market pricing, analyst consensus estimates, tariff policy assumptions, and supply-demand modeling.
