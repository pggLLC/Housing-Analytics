About Affordable Housing Intelligence

A comprehensive platform for understanding Low-Income Housing Tax Credit allocations, market dynamics, and affordable housing development across the United States.

Platform Purpose

Affordable Housing Intelligence provides data-driven insights into how federal housing tax credits are allocated, priced, and deployed across states and metro regions. Our platform serves affordable housing developers, tax credit investors, housing finance agencies, and community development professionals.

We consolidate data from authoritative sources to help users understand policy variations, market conditions, and development opportunities in the affordable housing sector.

Why LIHTC Matters

The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit program is the federal government's primary tool for encouraging private investment in affordable rental housing. Since 1986, LIHTC has financed approximately 3.6 million affordable rental units across all 50 states.

While federally authorized, LIHTC operates through a state-level allocation system. Each state housing finance agency designs its own Qualified Allocation Plan (QAP), creating distinct development environments, risk profiles, and investment outcomes across regions.

Core Sources

Economy & Inflation

  • Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) — economic indicators, interest rates, housing starts
  • BLS Producer Price Index (PPI) — construction input costs by material category
  • BLS Current Population Survey (CPS) — labor force and employment
  • BLS JOLTS — job openings and labor turnover

Census

  • American Community Survey (ACS) — population, housing, and income data
  • Census New Residential Construction — housing starts and permits

HUD

  • HUD QCT/DDA Designations — qualified census tracts and difficult development areas
  • HUD Income Limits — area median income limits by geography
  • HUD LIHTC Dataset (landing page) — project-level LIHTC allocation data
  • HUD LIHTC Query Tool — interactive project search

LIHTC & Policy

  • NCSHA — state housing finance agency information and LIHTC policy resources
  • Novogradac Affordable Housing Tax Credits Hub — market analysis and pricing data
  • Novogradac QCT/DDA Mapping Tool — interactive mapping tool

Colorado

  • CHFA — Colorado Housing and Finance Authority LIHTC program and allocations
  • DOLA Proposition 123 — local government affordable housing commitments

CRA / Finance

  • FFIEC CRA — Community Reinvestment Act data and disclosure reports
  • NCUA Grants & Community Development — credit union community development resources

Methodology

Data Aggregation: Data is consolidated from authoritative public sources and validated against multiple references to ensure accuracy.

State Allocations: Per-capita calculations use Census Bureau population estimates (ACS population or Census estimates as used on the page). Allocation tiers are based on total dollar amounts, not adjusted for cost of living or construction costs. Rankings are sorted descending by selected metric; ties follow stable sort / alphabetical where applicable.

Market Analysis: Pricing data reflects secondary market transactions and may vary by deal structure, investor type, and state-specific factors.

Economic Indicators: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) provides standardized metrics updated according to each series' publication schedule. CPI and PPI are index levels, not dollars; changes are computed on index levels. Charts show native units (SAAR, %, index, or dollars as labeled).

Commodity & Cost Indicators: Commodity and cost indicators are reported from BLS PPI series accessed via FRED. The site charts observed data and simple changes (e.g., year-over-year), not forward projections unless explicitly labeled.

Site Calculations

Year-over-Year % Change: (current − prior_year) / prior_year × 100

Per-Capita Allocations: allocation ÷ population using ACS population estimates or Census estimates as available for the geography shown.

Ranking Logic: Sorted descending by selected metric; ties follow stable sort / alphabetical where applicable.

Index Series: CPI and PPI are index levels, not dollar amounts. Changes are computed directly on index levels.

Units & Scaling: Charts show native units — SAAR (seasonally adjusted annual rate), percentage points, index levels, or dollars — as labeled on each chart.

Maps: Maps are rendered with Leaflet; basemap tiles may be sourced from OpenStreetMap contributors (per tile provider attribution).

Updates & Maintenance

Data Refresh: Data updates are automated via GitHub Actions where configured; refresh cadence varies by source/series. Some datasets update daily/weekly/monthly; others update when agencies publish new releases.

Economic Indicators: FRED series are fetched daily by a scheduled workflow and cached in data/fred-data.json; individual series update on FRED's own publication schedule.

Policy Changes: QAP updates tracked as state agencies publish new allocation plans.
