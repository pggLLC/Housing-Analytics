Affordable Housing Pipeline — Methodology

Where the data lives: the live pipeline — kanban, signal log, anti-targets, network, and outreach templates — moved to a private workspace in June 2026 and is no longer published in this repository. This page documents the methodology that the pipeline follows, so the ranking tools (Opportunity Finder, Jurisdiction Briefs) can be read in context.

What this is

A weekly-maintained pipeline of Colorado jurisdictions where we are actively pursuing housing development opportunities. The pipeline tracks the full lifecycle from "I noticed a signal" → "a contract is executed", plus the network of contacts, dropped targets, and re-usable outreach templates that support it.

The 5 stages (left-to-right on the kanban)

  1. Screen — passed the Opportunity Finder filter (above the IOI threshold) but no signal logged yet. Default landing column for new entries.
  2. Signal — at least one moderate-or-strong signal logged in the last 90 days (HNA refresh, RFP open, dedicated funding move, etc).
  3. Outreach — email sent or call scheduled in the last 30 days. Move here as soon as a real conversation is in flight.
  4. IC — feasibility analysis or Investment Committee packet underway; specific site / partner identified.
  5. Active — executed LOI / MOU / contract. The actual deal is live.

Demotion rule: any jurisdiction sitting 90+ days in a stage with no progress gets pushed one stage left with a note explaining why. A 6th stage, Monitor, exists for long-cycle relationships and is folded into the Screen column on the kanban.

The IOI score

The Opportunity Index (IOI) is a 0–100 composite computed by the LIHTC Opportunity Finder for every CO jurisdiction. It blends housing-need pressure, funding posture (Prop 123, PAB, dedicated revenue), civic capacity, basis-boost eligibility, and market-capture advantage. The full weighting is documented in LIHTC-LOCATOR-METHODOLOGY.md. We promote any jurisdiction above ~60 to at least the Screen stage.

Classifications (A / B / C / D)

  • A — real development opportunity, we act as primary developer.
  • B — planning-to-development consulting opportunity; fee work that may convert to A.
  • C — relationship / monitor; long-cycle but worth nurturing.
  • D — not worth current time; moved to Anti-Targets.

Confidence (high / medium / low)

  • high — in the COHO curated 33 + recent ACS/CHAS + ZORI or Apartment List coverage.
  • medium — recent ACS/CHAS but limited policy data.
  • low — sparse data; small CDP; no recent HNA. A low confidence entry should not be in Outreach without an explicit override note.

Signal strength (weak / moderate / strong)

  • weak — interesting but not actionable. Default for most rumors / generic council mentions.
  • moderate — actionable in 30–90 days (HNA draft expected, RFP cycle approaching, personnel change).
  • strong — drives a same-week action (open RFP < 60 days, immediate land disposition, direct partnership invite). If > 30 % of your signals are strong, you're inflating — recalibrate.

How pipeline entries connect to the public tools

  • Each pipeline entry links to its Jurisdiction Brief — a full data sheet with HNA status, AMI gap, LIHTC inventory, funding readiness, and civic scoring.
  • Each entry carries the signal-log sources (and their original URLs) that justified the pipeline placement. An entry with no sources is a flag — promote a signal or demote the entry.
  • The Opportunity Finder is the top of the funnel: its IOI ranking decides which jurisdictions enter the Screen stage at all.

Weekly review routine

Monday morning, walk the kanban left-to-right: demote stalled entries, promote new signals, add a next action + due date for each active row, then sweep the signal log for anything older than 90 days.

The datasets

Five datasets back the pipeline (maintained in the private workspace, not in this repository):

  • Signal log — every observed signal with date, source, source URL, strength.
  • Pipeline — the kanban itself (one row per jurisdiction in the funnel).
  • Anti-targets — explicitly dropped jurisdictions with reason + revisit date.
  • Network — people we know in each jurisdiction.
  • Outreach templates — re-usable email/letter templates.

A controlled vocabulary pins the allowed values for every column (stages, strengths, classifications, product types), and two guard rails run against the data: every geoid must resolve to a real Census place whose name matches the jurisdiction string, and every signal source URL is health-checked periodically so rotted links get flagged.
