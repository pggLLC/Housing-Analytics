LIHTC Opportunity Finder

Colorado jurisdictions (cities, towns, CDPs) with QCT (Qualified Census Tract) and/or DDA (Difficult Development Area) designations, ranked for 4% bond and 9% competitive LIHTC targeting. Sortable by last placed-in-service year, HNA need score, and population. Combines funding-recency, housing need (Scorecard v2 composite), basis-boost eligibility (IRC §42(d)(5)(B)), and population — re-weighted per round (4% bonds emphasise scale, 9% emphasises geographic gap).

Screening tool only. Opportunity scores are a starting point for site identification. Final LIHTC site selection requires a formal CHFA-required PMA, market study, and developer underwriting. All data is public (HUD LIHTC, HUD QCT/DDA designations, ACS, CHAS, TIGER place-tract spatial joins) — no proprietary signals.

How is the opportunity score calculated?

Each jurisdiction (city / town / CDP) with QCT and/or DDA designation gets a 0–100 composite score from four components. Weights re-tune by target round because 4% bond deals and 9% competitive deals optimise on different signals:

Component 9% Competitive 4% Bond Balanced
Recency40%25%35%
Housing need30%25%30%
Basis-boost20%15%20%
Population10%35%15%
  • Recency — years since the jurisdiction's most recent LIHTC project was placed in service (PROJ_CTY match against HUD LIHTC). Capped at 25+ years. Jurisdictions that have never been funded get 100. Signals saturation headroom — CHFA's geographic-distribution scoring rewards gap areas. Heaviest in 9% because gap-area scoring is a 9% QAP differentiator.
  • Housing need — Scorecard v2 composite for the containing county, expressed as a Colorado-wide percentile. Blends tenure-blended cost burden (renter pct_cb30 × renter HHs + owner pct_cb30 × owner HHs) and severe rent burden (≥50% income). Source: HUD CHAS Table 7.
  • Basis-boost — QCT only: 60 of 100, DDA only: 60, both: 100, neither: 0. IRC §42(d)(5)(B) basis boost is a single election, but jurisdictions with both designations have the strongest underwriting case (the default filter).
  • Population — bucketed: <500: 0, 500–2k: 30, 2k–5k: 60, 5k–15k: 85, ≥15k: 100. Heaviest in 4% bond because bond deals typically need 100–200 units to absorb, which requires a population base.

Sources: HUD LIHTC database (~716 CO projects, YR_PIS 1987–2020), HUD QCT designations (224 CO tracts), HUD DDA designations (10 CO nonmetro counties), data/hna/place-tract-membership.json (TIGER 2024 spatial join of CO places against tracts), HUD CHAS Table 7 for cost-burden composite, ACS DP04 for population proxy.

Caveats: (1) Projects with YR_PIS = 8888 (HUD placeholder for in-pipeline projects) are excluded. (2) Project-to-jurisdiction matching uses PROJ_CTY string equality — a project with a misspelled or unincorporated PROJ_CTY may not be counted. (3) Population is approximate: derived from CHAS households ≤100% AMI × 2.5 average household size, not direct ACS B01003. (4) QCT-place membership requires the tract's area share of the place to exceed 5% or the place's area share of the tract to exceed 20% — sliver-overlaps don't claim a QCT. (5) DDA designation in Colorado is county-based for the 10 nonmetro CO counties; all jurisdictions in those counties inherit the designation.
